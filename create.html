Your Title

The title of your piece is the first thing that people see, and with no front cover for your text - it is up to the title to pull the reader in and actually get them to read what you have written.

A Summary

When people find your text they want to know something about what goes on - don't reveal any big twists, but get the general idea of the text across. Consider it the blurb of your piece, it has to make people want to know more.

Your Name

It doesn't actually have to be your name, but it should be you. Your author name is how people find you specifically so it should be a common denominator. Make it clear, logical and suitable.

The Content

This is your actual text, you have between 250 and 300 words to get across your message - to make the reader laugh or cry, to kill or to save lives (though not literally). You can use basic markdown.

Some Tags

Try to pick 5 words, things that categorise your piece. People can and will find you by these tags - they are important and it is these that allow people to find you even if you have a strange title.
